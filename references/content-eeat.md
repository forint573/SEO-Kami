## What E-E-A-T actually is
E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust, with **Trust at the center** — the other three feed it. (PROVEN, Google docs) It is a concept from the Search Quality Rater Guidelines, used by human raters to evaluate result quality. Raters do NOT change rankings; their judgments train and validate Google's systems. So E-E-A-T is something Google's algorithms **approximate through many signals**, not a number attached to a page or site. (PROVEN) There is no "E-E-A-T score" to optimize toward; treat it as a design target for genuinely trustworthy content, and tell the user the same.

## Operationalizing each signal
Each lever below is a concrete, shippable signal — not a guarantee.

**Experience** (first-hand) — Show the author did/used/visited the thing: original photos, screenshots, test results, "I ran this for 6 months" detail, named conditions, things only someone present would know. (CONSENSUS) This is the differentiator AI-spun summaries cannot fake.

**Expertise** — Real author identity with byline, bio, and relevant credentials; topic depth and accuracy. Match the expertise type to the topic (formal credentials for medical/legal; lived expertise for hobbies/consumer reviews). (PROVEN/CONSENSUS)

**Authoritativeness** — Off-site reputation: being cited, quoted, mentioned, and linked by others in the field; recognition as a go-to source. Built off-page, not by self-declaration. (CONSENSUS)

**Trust** (central) — Accurate, current facts; transparent sourcing and citations; clear ownership, contact, and editorial/correction policy; no deception. Inaccurate or stale claims undercut everything else. (PROVEN/CONSENSUS)

Concrete checklist: real bylines linked to author pages with credentials and sameAs links; visible publish/update dates that reflect actual revisions; inline citations to primary sources; first-hand proof (media, data, specifics); accurate current facts; a real About/contact page; corroborating off-site mentions and reviews.

## YMYL extra care
"Your Money or Your Life" topics — health, finance, safety, legal, civic, major life decisions — get the highest scrutiny because errors can cause real harm. (PROVEN) Raise the bar: named, credentialed authors or reviewers; citations to authoritative primary sources; conservative, accurate, dated claims; clear medical/financial disclaimers where appropriate. Note the schema exception — FAQPage/HowTo lost general rich results, but authoritative **government and health** sites can still earn some FAQ enhancement. (PROVEN)

## Helpful Content folded into core
The standalone Helpful Content System was **folded into Google's core ranking systems** as of the March 2024 core update. (PROVEN) There is no separate "HCU" to recover from anymore — content quality is now assessed continuously inside core. Practical consequence: recovery from a quality-related decline follows the **slow core-update cadence** (months, tied to broad updates), not a quick fix. The self-assessment test stands: "Is this content people would find satisfying?" (PROVEN)

## What core updates reward and demote
**Reward:** unique, people-first content with a clear point of view; first-hand experience and original analysis; content that satisfies the searcher's intent and leaves them not needing to look further. (PROVEN/CONSENSUS)

**Demote:** commodity/recycled summaries that add nothing new; thin, AI-spun pages produced at scale with no added value; content made primarily for search engines rather than people; "search-first" pages padded with keywords. (PROVEN)

The line is not "AI vs. human" — Google judges quality and usefulness regardless of how content was produced. Mass-generated content with no original value is the problem, not automation per se. (PROVEN)

## Spam policies that demote
These are explicit Google spam policies (PROVEN); violating them can suppress or de-index:
- **Scaled content abuse** — producing many pages primarily to manipulate rankings and provide little value, at scale (manual or automated).
- **Site reputation abuse** ("parasite SEO") — publishing third-party content that trades on a host site's ranking signals with little host oversight (e.g., coupon/affiliate sections grafted onto an authoritative domain).
- **Expired-domain abuse** — buying an expired domain mainly to exploit its residual reputation for unrelated, low-value content.

## Anti-patterns to flag
- "Add author bios to get an E-E-A-T score boost" — there is no score; bios help only as part of genuine trust signals. (PROVEN)
- "Recover from the Helpful Content penalty quickly" — it is in core; recovery is slow and update-gated. (PROVEN)
- "AI content is automatically demoted" — false; only low-value/scaled abuse is. Quality is the test. (PROVEN)
- Faking experience (stock photos, invented anecdotes) — high-risk and counter to the Experience signal. (CONSENSUS)
